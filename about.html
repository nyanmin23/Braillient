Our Mission


We believe that everyone deserves a decent education including the disabled, which is sadly not the case in Myanmar.


There are many problems preventing disabled students from getting the same education as others.


One of them being not enough learning resources made with said disabilities in mind.


Our team decided to create the software to help visually impaired children get learning resources.


In this case, braille books.


There is no software, as far as we could find, that translates Myanmar text to Braille.


This causes a huge problem in visually impared students.
Learning English could be a hassle for some of them.
This is our motive behind this web-app.


While there are many softwares for smart education such as Flash Card Apps, To-do lists, Habit Trackers, Homework Trackers, Dashboard apps, none of these can be used by the visually impaired.

FAQs


1. How does the software work?
The software translates the Burmese(Myanmar) text into Braille language by using a converter. Click the button "Convert" and you're good to go!
2. How can this help visually impaired students?
Textbooks or material written in Myanmar language can now be easily translated into Braille formats. The formats are ready to be used by Braille embosser printers. The Braille text can then be printed out.
3. Can visually impaired people use this software?
Yes. We implemented texts alongside every button to ensure the screen reader application can capture the words for the user. Our design team has carefully sorted out by implementing both texts and audio cues. The user can easily navigate through buttons. We are not professional web-designers so, if you spot any mistakes please contact us and we'll sort it out!
